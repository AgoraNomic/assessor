            "text": "Amend Rule 911, \"Motions and Moots\", by replacing the following text:\n\n      If a judgement has been in effect for less then seven days and has\n      not been entered into Moot, then:\n\n      - The judge of that CFJ CAN self-file a Motion to Reconsider the\n        case by announcement, if e has not already self-filed a Motion\n        to Reconsider that CFJ.\n      - Any Player CAN group-file a Motion to Reconsider the case with 2\n        support, if the CFJ has not had a Motion to Reconsider\n        group-filed for it at any time while it has been assigned to its\n        current judge.\n\n      When a Motion to Reconsider is so filed, the case is rendered open\n      again.\n\n      If a CFJ has a judgement assigned, a player CAN enter that\n      judgement into Moot with N+2 support, where N is the number of\n      weeks since that judgement has been assigned, rounded down.\n\nwith:\n\n      The judge of a CFJ CAN by announcement self-file a Motion to\n      Reconsider it, if its most recent judgement has been in effect for\n      less than seven days.\n\n      Any player CAN with 2 support group-file a Motion to Reconsider\n      a case, if its most recent judgement had been in effect for less\n      than seven days at the time the intent was tabled.\n\n      The above notwithstanding, it is IMPOSSIBLE to file a Motion to\n      Reconsider a CFJ whose current judgement has been entered into\n      Moot, or that has already had a Motion to Reconsider filed against\n      it by the same method since it was most recently assigned to a\n      judge.\n\n      When a Motion to Reconsider a case is filed, the case is rendered\n      open again.\n\n      If a CFJ has a judgement assigned, a player CAN enter that\n      judgement into Moot with N+2 support, where N is the number of\n      weeks from the time the judgement was assigned to the time the\n      intent was tabled, rounded down.\n\nCOMMENTS:\n\nThis is attempting to fix the support requirement for Moots so that it\ncan be predicted at the time the intent is tabled. Currently, it's\ndependent on the age of the judgement at the time the intent is\n*resolved*, so at the time it's tabled there are several possible values\n- and each must be intended individually, because the support\nrequirement has to be explicitly stated at the time the intent is\ntabled.\n\nFor consistency, I changed the support requirement for Motions to\nReconsider in the same way (even though this is not troublesome as-is)."
